    Happiness is a bank

    Happiness should be like the push-pull of a flywheel. In the good times, you don’t indulge, you deposit. If you’re happier more than you’re sad, then the well never empties—in fact it should monotonically increase.

    • This is the only way to stop your happiness from being hedonic.
    • Why is it so hard to be happy with what we have and got, and then channel that happiness into working towards what you don’t yet have.
    • Our blessings include not just what we have but what we got.
    • What we have is transient, ephemeral, and infinitesimal.

    Imagine enjoying what you have today versus enjoying what you’ve HAD (a stickman standing compounding giant graph), then on the right, you have the scaries of life.

    Would you rather be standing on a thin column or a big ass bunker of a graph?

    Why do we party - if not to cultivate a persistent bank of happiness? It can’t be just to enjoy the present moment, just today.

    How?

    • Whenever you experience ecstasy, your first job is to feel less.
    • If you can’t compress it, then don’t let it fizzle out! Take a moment, breath it in, and then cup as much of it up as you can and put it in a box.
    • This is the box you keep to get you through the bad times, where you can take things out one by one.
    • Life is a sine wave with noise. Hold onto your box

    There are two different modes of experience:

    1. Experience to hoard memories
    2. Experience to experience

    Watch Naval’s biggest mistake thing

    Cache your happiness desperately. Why desperately? Because happiness actively working against you.

    Caching is a prerequisite to practicing high quality gratitude.

    You don’t spent all your money as soon you receive it - instead put it in the bank and let it compound.

    Instead of experiencing happiness reactively, we should treat happiness like money and only withdraw it when we need it.

    When you feel happiness…

    1. Notice it
    2. Store it Cache your happiness

    Low-GI happiness

    Slow down your happiness Acknowledge when you’re in abundance, and have the proactive discipline not to get ahead of yourself

    To paraphrase bill gates: success is a lousy teacher. It seduced you into thinking you couldn’t lose

    Tend to the garden of things that do not need to be tended to yet. Be grossly proactive, be unreasonably overprepared.

    No matter what. Stay disciplined. 

    When you get wealthy you reinvest. Don’t spend. So Don’t spend your happiness. - it will pay off and lead to more long term.

    “You have nothing yet.”

    Do not celebrate. It is the most dangerous thing to do.

    Links to gratitude. That perfect tension between ‘I have everything’ and ‘I have nothing.’

    Seeing the fruits of success should impel you to go back to that dark place

    Gratitude. Pray for more meals. You can always break down things into atomic units. The unit is gratitude. God gives you blessings not happiness.

    Over enough iterations you will start to notice the lifecycle, how effort leads to reward. Hopefully, you will eventually learn to focus on the effort side of the equation, to see reward only as a derivative effort; and thus to focus only on the effort side of the equation, not the reward; go as far as to ignore it completely.

    Mimesis

    If you are constantly pursuing happiness then you are, by definition, unhappy most of the time. 

    Admitting you don’t know mimesis will lead you down a great adventure.

    Be very selective about what you derive happiness from. Be very intentional about it. Any time you do this, you create a dependency.
