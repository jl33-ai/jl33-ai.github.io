    Thesis: ONLY BECAUSE I LOVE YOU

    This is a great model for extreme people, and offers a remedial framework that leverages (gets more effective with) one’s extremity.

    This is only one suggested archetype 

    Life is about finding a framework upon which to build your perfect self - and then when to make exceptions. Exceptions have no weight unless they are overriding something of Great significanc

    In a sense the only thing we set out to do is build the best framework for the self. And fulfill it 

    This arises in ‘we call it colloquially self discovery or knowing thy self. this gets us close to a candidate for the meaning of life* but not quite. The necessary missing ingredient is obviously what is colloquially known as living a little. For the sake of life, which is really just a proxy for lovey

    From my father; a simple phrase uttered every single morning. Back when I was impressionable, my sole task for the day. ‘always be the best you can be.’ Most of the time, he was beyond my line of sight. I would simply hear: ‘always be the best you can be.’ 

    These two axioms exist in beautiful tension.

    Without a sufficiently bedrock framework to protect, this means there is no beauty in overriding it. This is why sometimes we end up loving the villain more after they do one singular redeeming them - even though the Hero has obviously done more net good.

    We are hard wired to seek beauty in change, in contrast. 

    1. Without love, you are an empty machine. You are boring. You are, on paper, definitionally perfect. And yet you lack what we call ;life; 

    2. Like a film where nothing ever goes wrong - where there is no conflict to overcome. Homogeneity. 

    You’ll realise. It’s the best thing you’ll ever say. Sigh. Only because I love you 

    This story begins with the audacious declaration that  

    What do I mean by this framework?

    We all have some ideal version of ourselves - some [utopian] version which moves, reacts, speaks

    Those who are simply happy with who they already are have found their framework and fulfilled it. 

    It explains why we are, by default. So sensitive to what we think others are thinking about us, or are obsessed with other people whose frameworks we are trying to be more like.   

    Thus life is the pursuit of this framework; both its design and then subsequent fulfillment. 

    We all harbour some. 

    We naturally gravitate to those who have a well established idea of what their self framework is, and are religious in its upkeeping. We call it self-assured. They emit a sense of stability that we so deeply desire. They seem able to defend effortlessly their choices, and simply hearing about the consistency of their… satiates some deep-seated craving for metamorphosis  and reliantly 

    Fast & pray before you eat 

    Life is about building a bedrock and transcendental framework for life, and then having the [life] within you to abort it for morals

    • Life is _ to pursue your ideal framework for the self.
