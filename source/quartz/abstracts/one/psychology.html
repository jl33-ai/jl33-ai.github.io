    Speak the language of psychology (maybe make this a folder cos it’s so interesting)

    1. Social Proof: People tend to conform to the actions of others under the assumption that those actions are reflective of the correct behavior.

    2. Blushing is Involuntary: Blushing is an automatic physiological response controlled by the sympathetic nervous system and cannot be consciously controlled.

    3. The Halo Effect: An initial positive impression of someone can influence how we perceive other aspects of their character, often overlooking negatives.

    4. Confirmation Bias: Individuals favor information that confirms their existing beliefs and discount information that challenges them.

    5. The Bystander Effect: The more people who witness an emergency, the less likely any one person is to help, assuming others will intervene.

    6. Cognitive Dissonance: Experiencing discomfort from holding conflicting beliefs, leading individuals to adjust their beliefs or actions to reduce the inconsistency.

    7. The Dunning-Kruger Effect: People with limited knowledge or competence in a domain often overestimate their own ability.

    8. Classical Conditioning: Learning through association, where a neutral stimulus becomes associated with a meaningful stimulus, eliciting a conditioned response.

    9. Operant Conditioning: Learning through consequences, where behaviors are strengthened or weakened by rewards or punishments.

    10. The Placebo Effect: A patient’s symptoms can improve after receiving a treatment with no therapeutic value, due to their belief in the treatment.

    11. Heuristics: Mental shortcuts or rules of thumb that simplify decision-making but can sometimes lead to cognitive biases.

    12. The Mere Exposure Effect: Repeated exposure to a stimulus increases an individual’s preference for it.

    13. Learned Helplessness: After repeated exposure to uncontrollable events, individuals may become passive and accept situations they could change.

    14. The Spotlight Effect: People tend to overestimate how much others notice and remember about their appearance and actions.

    15. Emotional Intelligence: The ability to recognize, understand, manage, and reason with emotions in oneself and others.

    16. Positive Reinforcement: Encouraging the repetition of a behavior by immediately following it with a desirable outcome.

    17. Ego Depletion: Self-control or willpower draws upon a limited pool of mental resources that can be used up.

    18. Flow State: A mental state of complete immersion and focus in an activity, often leading to high levels of creativity and productivity.

    19. The Pygmalion Effect: Higher expectations from others can lead to improved performance, while low expectations can hinder it.

    20. Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs: A theory proposing that humans are motivated by a hierarchy of needs, starting from basic physiological needs to self-actualization.
